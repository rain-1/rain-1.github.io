Lean declarations

  • Zeta5Odd.zeta_odd_irrational

There exists \(j\in \texttt{oddIdx}\) with \(\zeta (j)\) irrational; that is, at least one of \(\zeta (5),\zeta (7),\dots ,\zeta (33)\) is irrational.

Proof ▼

Assume every \(\zeta (j)\), \(j\in \texttt{oddIdx}\), is rational and let \(a{\gt}0\) be a common denominator (Lemma 19). Put \(b_n:=d_n^{33}(7r_n-\hat r_n)\). By Theorem 16 and Lemma 11, \(a\, b_n\) is a positive integer, so \(a\, b_n\ge 1\). On the other hand the root test — Lemma 12 for the decay rate \(g(x_0)\), Hanson’s bound \(d_n\le 3^n\) (Theorem 17) and the numeric margin \(3^{33}g(x_0){\lt}1\) (Lemma 18) — gives \(b_n\to 0\), hence \(a\, b_n{\lt}1\) eventually. Contradiction.
