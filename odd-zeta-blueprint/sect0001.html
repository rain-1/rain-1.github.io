1 Overview

This blueprint charts the Lean 4 / Mathlib formalization of W. Zudilin’s elementary theorem (arXiv:1801.09895), in the \(s=33\) variant: at least one of \(\zeta (5),\zeta (7),\dots ,\zeta (33)\) is irrational. The whole development is sorry-free and uses only the standard axioms propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound; every box in the dependency graph is green.

The proof has three arithmetic–analytic strands that meet in the final contradiction:

  • an asymptotic strand (Zudilin’s Lemma 4): the well-poised sums \(r_n,\hat r_n\) have a controlled exponential decay rate \(g(x_0)\);

  • an integrality strand (Zudilin’s Lemmas 1–3): a \(\mathbb {Z}\)-linear combination \(d_n^{33}(7r_n-\hat r_n)=\sum _j A_j\zeta (j)+A_0\);

  • Hanson’s elementary bound \(d_n\le 3^n\) on \(d_n=\operatorname {lcm}(1,\dots ,n)\).

A root test then forces a nonzero integer into \((0,1)\).
